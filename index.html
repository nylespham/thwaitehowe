About Thwaite Howe

a Victorian family country home with ten bedrooms in an ideal Lake District village setting

Introduction

Thwaite Howe is a large, traditional, Victorian Lakeland stone and state detached house set in an elevated position at the edge of Thornthwaite village in an acre of private garden. It benefits from a magnificent view across the Derwent valley towards Skiddaw, Little Man and Latrigg.

The house was built around 1860 for a retiring businessman from Manchester. It was renovated in 2016 by the current owners.

Thwaite Howe is a family home operating as a large holiday home under professional, experienced management. It is ideal for anniversarys and other family and group gatherings.

Review the photo gallery and contact us if you would like further information regarding availability for short term holiday letting.
